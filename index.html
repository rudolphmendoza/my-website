South Korea

South Korea offers a rich blend of traditional culture, modern technology, natural beauty, and exciting food scenes. I was stationed there from 2013-2015, but my wife and kids were not with me. This is one of my most favorite places to go. The people are wonderful, the food is delicious, and the transit system is super easy to understand. It also celebrates one of the best cherry blossom festivals.

N Seoul Tower / Namsan Tower / Seoul Tower

Seoul's most iconic landmarks, offering panoramic views of the city from the top of Namsan Mountain. It is similar to the Space Needle in Seattle. There is a steep walk going up towards the base of the tower. Get ready for a killer exercise for your calves! For couples, you can bring a padlock and secure it to some of the railings around the tower. This symbolizes eternal love and commitment between couples.

Everland

Everland is South Korea's largest theme park and the equivalent to Disneyland. Filled with rides, food, and entertainment, you're sure to have plenty of fun! I was assigned to a very small and hidden base near Everland called Yongin. How I wish that I would have snuck off to visit this resort!

Japan

One of the most sought after festivals is Japan's Cherry Blossom in the Spring. It's funny how I've only been here for a layover plenty of times but have never explored this beautiful country. It's never too late!

Germany

My wife has been there, but we would love to travel there as a family. I've always wanted to go there especially during my time in the military. Most of my coworkers have told me of the great times they had there. I've been missing out!
